Is it Feasible to Become a Remote Video Game Tester?

August 17, 2023 Author: Ava Lopez

Are you an avid gamer with a passion for playing video games? If so, you might have wondered if there’s a way to turn your love for gaming into a potential career path. One intriguing avenue to explore is becoming a remote video game tester. This exciting opportunity allows you to combine your gaming skills with work, but is it truly feasible? Let’s delve into the details to find out.

Table Of Contents
  1. The Role of a Remote Video Game Tester
  2. The Pros of Remote Game Testing
  3. The Challenges to Consider
  4. Steps to Become a Remote Video Game Tester
  5. Conclusion

The Role of a Remote Video Game Tester

Before we delve into the feasibility of becoming a remote video game tester, let’s first understand what this role entails. As a game tester, your primary responsibility is to play through video games to identify bugs, glitches, and other issues that could affect the gaming experience for players. Your feedback helps developers improve the game’s quality before it’s released to the public.

The Pros of Remote Game Testing

Remote game testing comes with several enticing advantages. First and foremost, you get to work from the comfort of your own home or location of your choice. This eliminates the need for a daily commute and allows for a flexible work schedule. Additionally, remote testing can provide access to a wide range of game titles, offering diversity in your testing experience.

Flexibility and Convenience

Remote game testing offers unparalleled flexibility. You can often choose when you want to work, making it an ideal option for individuals with other commitments, such as students or parents.

Exposure to Game Development

Engaging in game testing provides you with a unique opportunity to witness the game development process firsthand. Your feedback is crucial to the game’s improvement, making you an essential part of the creative cycle.

The Challenges to Consider

While the idea of playing games and getting paid for it sounds appealing, it’s essential to acknowledge the challenges that come with the role.

High Competition

The gaming industry is competitive, and landing a remote testing gig can be challenging. Companies often have specific requirements, and the applicant pool can be large.

Attention to Detail

Game testing requires a keen eye for detail. You’ll need to identify and document even the smallest glitches, ensuring the game’s quality before release.

Steps to Become a Remote Video Game Tester

If you’re still interested in pursuing a career as a remote game tester, here are some steps to consider:

1. Build Gaming Experience

Develop a strong gaming skillset across various genres and platforms. This experience will be valuable when identifying issues during testing.

2. Research Companies

Look for companies that offer remote game testing positions. Research their requirements and application process.

3. Develop Testing Skills

Learn about the testing process, bug reporting, and industry terminology. These skills are essential for effective game testing.

4. Apply and Showcase Your Skills

Create a compelling application that highlights your gaming expertise and attention to detail. Some companies may require a test assignment to assess your skills.

Conclusion

While becoming a remote video game tester is an appealing prospect for many gamers, it’s essential to approach the role with realistic expectations. The industry can be competitive, and the job requires careful attention to detail. If you’re dedicated, persistent, and have a passion for both gaming and quality assurance, a career as a remote game tester could be a feasible and rewarding path.
